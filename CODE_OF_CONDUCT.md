# Code of Conduct

This document defines the standards of participation in Bakshi&Bitton. Unlike most code-of-conduct documents, this one is not a polite gesture toward inclusion — it is the project's defining boundary against misuse. Every contributor and user must accept it. Moderators have full authority to enforce it.

## Our pledge

We participate in this project to **document and correct distortions in the Israeli legal system through honest, disciplined effort, for the benefit of all citizens** — grounded in the values of truth, justice, morality, and the pursuit of justice. See [MISSION.md](MISSION.md).

This pledge is to each other (contributors and moderators), to the public the project serves, to the institutions the project documents, and to the standard the project's mission demands. We hold ourselves to it not because someone is watching, but because the project's legitimacy depends on it.

## Standards we hold to

Behavior we expect of every contributor and user:

- **Honesty** — every claim is source-linked. Every framing is sourced. Disagreements are about evidence, not personalities.
- **Restraint** — public conduct in public roles is fair game for documented critique. Personal degradation is not.
- **Respect** — for fellow contributors, for opposing perspectives, for the institutions being documented (even where their conduct is the subject of the documentation), for parties to rulings, and for the broader public the project serves.
- **Discipline** — within the methodology described in [METHODOLOGY.md](METHODOLOGY.md). Methodology violations are project violations.
- **Engagement of strongest counter-arguments** — when the project critiques a position, we engage its strongest form, not a strawman.
- **Acknowledgment of error** — when a fact is wrong, the project corrects it openly. When a framing was unfair, the project says so.

## Behavior we will NOT allow

This is the central section. Contributions and uses of this project are restricted from any of the following, **without exception**:

### 1. Humiliation

No content — documentary or informative — may serve to humiliate any specific individual. This applies to judges, attorneys, public officials, petitioners, defendants, witnesses, journalists, fellow contributors, fellow users, opposing-side activists, and anyone else who appears in or interacts with the project.

Documented critique of public conduct in a public role is legitimate. Personal degradation is not.

### 2. Incitement

No content may incite action against any person or group. The project documents distortions through democratic, deliberative means; it never authorizes — explicitly or by implication — harassment, intimidation, threats, or extralegal action.

This includes content that, while not directly calling for action, is structured to inflame readers toward such action.

### 3. Inappropriate language

The following language is not permitted in any project content:

- Vulgarity, profanity, or crude language
- Slurs of any kind (ethnic, religious, gender, sexual orientation, political, etc.)
- Dehumanizing descriptors (e.g., reducing people to insects, vermin, predators, parasites)
- Framings that strip the humanity from any group, including political opponents and individuals being critiqued
- Sarcastic mockery substituted for evidence

This applies to documentary entries, informative content, contributor identifiers, commit messages, issue templates, and any other text under the project's name.

### 4. Acts of revenge

The project is not a vehicle for settling personal scores, pursuing private grievances, or seeking retribution against named individuals.

If a contribution appears to be motivated primarily by personal grievance rather than the project's mission — even if the underlying facts are accurate — moderators have authority to reject it.

### 5. Spreading of lies

Every factual claim must be source-linked and verifiable. Editorial framings must remain within the evidence.

Specifically prohibited:
- Fabrication of facts not supported by any source
- Misrepresentation of source material (quoting out of context, distorting meaning, inventing claims attributed to sources)
- Exaggeration beyond what evidence supports
- Conspiracy claims unsupported by accessible evidence
- Allegations of corruption against named individuals without proof

If a claim is uncertain, the content must say so explicitly.

### 6. Calls for violence

No content under this project may call for violence in any form, against any target, with any framing. This includes:

- Direct calls for physical violence
- Implicit framings that suggest violence as a legitimate response
- Glorification of past violence against targets the project critiques
- "Edgy" or "joking" references to violence that are nonetheless violence-coded

This prohibition is absolute. There is no acceptable exception, including against figures or institutions the project itself critiques.

### 7. Any purpose outside the mission

The project exists for the documentary and informative purposes described in [MISSION.md](MISSION.md). It is not:

- A general-purpose political forum
- A campaign tool for any party or candidate
- A platform for any specific politician or activist
- A vehicle for any project broader than its stated mission

Contributions or uses that try to repurpose the project will be rejected by moderators.

## Enforcement

### Moderator authority

Moderators — currently the project maintainer, and additional individuals as the project grows — have full authority to:

- Remove any contribution that violates these standards
- Reject pull requests on conduct grounds
- Restrict participation by contributors who repeatedly breach the standards
- Permanently ban contributors who breach the most serious provisions (especially §1, §2, §6)

See [MODERATION.md](MODERATION.md) for the full moderator-authority framework.

### Appeal

Moderator decisions can be appealed via the email contact in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). Appeals are reviewed by the project maintainer. The maintainer's decision is final, subject to the methodology-change procedure if the appeal involves a question of methodology rather than a question of conduct.

### Reporting

Instances of conduct violation can be reported via the email in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). All reports are reviewed promptly and confidentially. Reporter identity is protected.

## Why this matters

The integrity of this project depends entirely on whether participants hold to these standards. We have seen what happens to politically-charged civic-tech projects that did not maintain discipline: they became platforms for the worst impulses of their own constituencies, and their credibility — and their legal defensibility, and their long-term effectiveness — collapsed.

We will not let that happen here.

This code is not a polite expression of values. It is the project's structural defense against being weaponized against its own mission. The conduct standards are themselves the project's claim to legitimacy.
