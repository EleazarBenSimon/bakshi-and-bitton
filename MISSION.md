# Mission

## What we are doing

Bakshi&Bitton exists to **preserve and distribute the record** of the continuous process by which the Israeli Supreme Court has been transformed — through doctrinal expansion since 1995 and accelerated in the post-2023 period — from a guardian of law into an instrument that constrains the elected institutions of the State of Israel against the will of the public who elected them.

We do this in two layers:

**The documentary core**: structured, source-linked records of every Supreme Court ruling that intervenes in a government decision or appointment. The facts here are verifiable. They are not in dispute; they are simply not gathered anywhere accessible to ordinary citizens, and they are not connected in a way that lets the larger pattern be seen.

**The informative content layer**: essays, explainers, pattern documents that connect individual rulings into the picture they form. Why this ruling matters in the context of the broader process. How this pattern of doctrinal expansion happened. What the consequences are for the elected institutions and ordinary citizens. This layer is moderator-reviewed; every claim traces back to the documentary core or to other verifiable sources.

## Who we are doing this for

- **Israeli citizens** who want to understand what has been done in their name, by institutions they did not elect, against the institutions they did
- **Journalists** who need quick access to sourced facts about specific rulings, patterns, and timelines
- **Advocacy organizations** working to restore constitutional balance through democratic means
- **Researchers and academics** who study the Israeli judicial system empirically
- **Citizens of any political persuasion** who are willing to engage with documented evidence rather than slogans

We are not doing this for any individual political party, for any individual politician, for any donor, or for any narrow interest group. We are doing it because the documented record matters, the larger picture matters, and ordinary citizens deserve access to both.

## What we are not doing

We are explicitly not doing — and will not allow anyone using this resource to do — any of the following:

- **Humiliating** any individual judge, attorney, public official, party to a ruling, or fellow participant. Documented criticism of public conduct in public roles is legitimate; personal degradation is not.
- **Inciting** action against any person or group. Documentation supports democratic remedy through legitimate means; it never authorizes harassment, threat, or extralegal action.
- **Using inappropriate language** — including vulgarity, slurs, ethnic or religious dehumanization, or framings that reduce humans to enemies.
- **Settling scores or pursuing personal revenge** through the documentary record. The record exists to serve the public good, not to gratify any individual's grievances.
- **Spreading falsehoods**. Every factual claim must be source-linked. Editorial framings that go beyond the source material will be rejected by moderators regardless of how compelling they sound.
- **Calling for violence** in any form, against any target, with any framing — including against figures or institutions the project itself critiques.
- **Operating outside** the project's defined purpose. This is not a general-purpose right-wing forum, a partisan campaign tool, a Bibist platform, an anti-judicial platform, or a vehicle for any project broader than the one described in this document.

## Why moderation matters

The integrity of this project depends entirely on whether the people who participate in it can hold to its standards. We have seen what happens to other politically-charged civic-tech projects that did not maintain discipline: they became platforms for the worst impulses of their own constituencies, and their credibility — and their legal defensibility, and their long-term effectiveness — collapsed.

We will not let that happen here. The moderators have full authority to remove content and contributors that fail the standards described in [METHODOLOGY.md](METHODOLOGY.md), [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md), and this document. Moderator decisions can be appealed (see [MODERATION.md](MODERATION.md)) but cannot be overridden by appeal to political alignment, audience preference, or popularity of the rejected content.

## The values this rests on

- **Truth** — verifiable, source-linked, and defensible. Every claim has a reference. Every reference has a citation. The Israeli Supreme Court's own published rulings, the official record at supreme.court.gov.il, and the related authoritative documents are the foundation on which everything in this project stands.

- **Justice** — the restoration of proper constitutional balance, in which elected institutions govern within the boundaries of the law and the law itself is determined by the consent of the governed. This is not "victory of one side over the other"; it is a return to democratic norms that benefit all citizens, including those who currently believe they benefit from the current distortion.

- **Morality** — the conduct of the project must itself reflect the principles it seeks to restore. We cannot demand fair process, restraint, and honesty from public institutions while exhibiting the opposite ourselves. The conduct standards in this project are themselves the project's claim to legitimacy.

- **Pursuit of justice for all citizens** — not the narrow interests of any political camp; not revenge; not factional victory. Sustainable peace and stability in which the State of Israel can serve all of its people require that the legal system serves the people rather than ruling over them.

## What success looks like

Three concrete markers:

1. **The documentary core becomes a routine citation** by Israeli journalists, lawyers, and academics across the political spectrum, including those who disagree with the project's editorial framing. The data is so clearly factual and well-sourced that it is used regardless of whether the user agrees with the larger argument.

2. **The informative content layer raises public awareness** of patterns that were previously visible only to specialists — making the systematic nature of the process intelligible to ordinary citizens who can then make informed democratic choices.

3. **The conduct of contributors and users** reflects the values described here. The project is known for the quality of its evidence, the restraint of its language, the fairness of its framing, and the discipline of its moderation — not for shouting matches, harassment campaigns, or factional tribalism.

If we achieve these, the project has fulfilled its purpose. If we do not, the project has failed regardless of its audience size or political traction.
