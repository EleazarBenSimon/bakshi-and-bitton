בג"ץ 8987/22

התנועה למען איכות השלטון בישראל ואח' נ' שר המשפטים ואח' (עתירות מאוחדות: בג"ץ 99/23, בג"ץ 179/23, בג"ץ 1711/24, בג"ץ 896/24)

הרכב של שלושה שופטים (וילנר, גרוסקופף, שטיין) הוציא פה אחד צו מוחלט המחייב את שר המשפטים יריב לוין לכנס את הוועדה לבחירת שופטים לבחירת נשיא קבוע לבית המשפט העליון, ולפרסם ברשומות את רשימת המועמדים לתפקיד תוך 14 ימים. בית המשפט קבע כי סעיף 7(א) לחוק בתי המשפט מטיל על השר חובה לפעול לשם מינוי שופטים, וכי מדיניות השר להתנות את כינוס הוועדה בהשגת הסכמה רחבה מאיינת את תכלית החוק.

סימני התנגדות
Following the September 12, 2024 mandatory order, Levin did not publish the candidate list or convene the Committee for a presidential vote within the 14-day window. On December 12, 2024, the High Court held a follow-up hearing on a petition to find Levin in contempt; the panel (including Acting President Amit) declined to hold Levin in contempt, characterizing contempt as reserved for extreme circumstances and noting some procedural steps had been taken, but ordered that a vote on a permanent Supreme Court president be held in the Committee by January 16, 2025. The Committee convened and elected Justice Yitzhak Amit as President on January 26, 2025; Levin was absent from that session. הערות

Structural decision: entered as a SINGLE ruling keyed to the primary mandatory order of September 12, 2024 (HCJ 8987/22 leads a cluster of consolidated petitions including HCJ 99/23, HCJ 179/23, HCJ 1711/24, HCJ 896/24). The December 12, 2024 follow-up ruling — which declined to hold Levin in contempt but issued an additional deadline — is documented in defiance_signals rather than as a separate ruling, because (a) it is procedurally subordinate to the September 12 mandatory order (a compliance-enforcement step within the same proceeding rather than an independent disposition on the merits), and (b) it would not be analytically parallel to the project's other 'Court declined' entry (Zini, 427/10/25), where the Court declined to intervene on the merits of an appointment; here the Court declined a remedial tool while reaffirming the underlying mandatory order. Panel composition varied across sittings in the cluster: the September 12, 2024 absolute order was issued by Willner, Grosskopf, and Stein; the December 12, 2024 follow-up panel included Acting President Yitzhak Amit and additional justices. Vote was unanimous on substance across all sittings. Compliance was achieved on January 26, 2025, when the Committee convened and elected Justice Amit as President; Levin did not attend that session. The official URL points to a court document filed January 2, 2025 within the case file (NetVerdicts/2025/1/2/2022-0-8987-62-2), which appears to be a procedural record in the 8987/22 docket. Doctrine: 'ultra_vires' is used here for the Court's finding that the Minister failed to exercise a mandatory statutory power under section 7(a) of the Courts Law (closest mapping in the controlled vocabulary to ministerial-duty-to-exercise-statutory-authority); 'reasonableness' captures the Court's secondary analysis of the Minister's stated policy of conditioning convening on broad consensus. Separation-of-powers and judicial-independence framings discussed in the underlying petitions are not separately enumerable in the project's doctrine vocabulary.
