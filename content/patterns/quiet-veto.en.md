# Reading Down: when the Court empties a law of force — not just when it strikes one

A law the Court **empties of content** is a law **cancelled in practice**. After the elected Knesset's huge effort to legislate, the statute stays on the books — but its power is quietly transferred to unelected hands, against the intent for which it was passed. The damage to the public's control over its own laws is the same — sometimes worse, because it is done silently. This is a public library of real, verified cases — each with a docket, date, panel, and authoritative source.

**48 verified cases** (1964–2025) · **25 read down / gutted (not formally struck)** · **23 struck or partly struck**. Full data: `data/library/quiet-veto-cases.json`.


> **What to call it.** The plainest, most established English term for this is **reading down** (Hebrew: **פרשנות מצמצמת**) — construing a statute so narrowly that almost none of its force survives, *without ever striking it down*. We lead with that term on purpose: the Court thrives on tangled language, so we answer in the simplest accurate words the field already uses. *Related terms, kept secondary:* **objective purposive interpretation** — Aharon Barak's method of reading a law to mean what the judge thinks it *ought* to mean; it is the engine of the practice and a larger story we treat on its own. And **the Quiet Veto**, our shorthand, which borrows a word from Aviad Bakshi — who calls the Court's power to kill a law a "veto" (JNS, 2020): this is that veto's silent face. Tellingly, no settled English name exists for the practice — and that is part of the story.

## Read down — emptied of force without being struck (reading down · פרשנות מצמצמת)


### HCJ 195/64 — HCJ 195/64 Southern Co. (Marbek Slaughterhouse) v. Chief Rabbinical Council (מרבק נ' המועצה הרבנית הראשית) (1964-09-27)

*Read down / gutted* · panel: Olshan P., Agranat D.P., Landau J., Witkon J., Manny J.


The Chief Rabbinical Council's statutory kashrut-certification authority (pre-1983) was left textually intact — but the unelected Court read it down, holding that licence conditions unrelated to the meat's actual kosher status were ultra vires. The text survived; its real-world reach was hollowed out, stripping the religious authority of power to enforce broader observance on the non-observant and confining it to the food itself.


**Statute:** Statutory authority of the Chief Rabbinical Council to grant kashrut certification (pre-1983 kashrut authority)  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/marbek-slaughter-house-v-chief-rabbinical-council)


### בג"ץ 465/89 (HCJ 465/89) — Raskin v. Religious Council of Jerusalem (1990)

*Read down / gutted*


The Knesset's Kashrut (Prohibition of Fraud) Law, 1983 was about food kashrut alone. Without voiding a single word, the Court read it down to that narrow purpose, holding that the power to grant a kashrut certificate is not a lever for imposing rules of Jewish law unrelated to the food — stripping the Rabbinate of the licensing leverage it had used to condition certification on barring "immodest" belly-dance performances. An unelected bench hollowed the statute's real-world reach below the Rabbinate's design, on its own interpretive say-so.


**Statute:** חוק איסור הונאה בכשרות, התשמ"ג-1983 — Kashrut (Prohibition of Fraud) Law, 1983  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/raskin-v-religious-council-jerusalem)


### דנ"פ 2316/95 — Ganimat v. State of Israel (Further Hearing) (1995)

*Read down / gutted*


Criminal Procedure Law (sec. 21a): the Knesset let courts jail defendants pre-trial on a fear-to-public-safety ground, which prosecutors turned into near-automatic detention by branding whole crime categories a "national scourge." Without voiding a single word, the Court read the provision down "in the spirit of" Basic Law: Human Dignity and Liberty — holding a national scourge alone is no detention ground; the state must prove the individual defendant's danger. Seven unelected justices hollowed out the legislature's intent.


**Statute:** Criminal Procedure Law [Consolidated Version], 5742-1982 — detention-until-end-of-proceedings ground, section 21a(a)(1) (סעיף 21א(א)(1)). (Reported PD 49(4) 589.)  

**Source:** [Wikipedia](https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D7%A2%D7%99%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%93_%D7%92%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%AA_%D7%A0'_%D7%9E%D7%93%D7%99%D7%A0%D7%AA_%D7%99%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%90%D7%9C)


### בג"ץ 4562/92 — Zandberg v. Broadcasting Authority (1996)

*Read down / gutted*


The Broadcasting Authority (Validation of Radio and TV Fees) Law, 5752-1992 — which the Knesset passed to retroactively validate fee collection an earlier ruling had voided. Barak did not strike the law; he read it down, refusing to apply its retroactive reach to arrears fines on property-rights and no-penalty-in-arrears grounds. This is where the doctrine was minted — "better to narrow a statute by interpretation than to declare part of it void" — the engine by which an unelected court hollows out the elected legislature's law without voiding a single word of text.


**Statute:** Broadcasting Authority Law, 5725-1965; Price Stability Law (Temporary Provision), 5745-1985; and the Broadcasting Authority (Validation of Radio and Television Fees) Law, 5752-1992. (Reported PD 50(2) 793.)  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/association-civil-rights-israel-v-israel-police)


### בג"ץ 3267/97 (HCJ 3267/97, joined with HCJ 715/98) — Rubinstein v. Minister of Defense (1998)

*Read down / gutted*


The Defense Service Law (5746-1986): without voiding a single word, the Court read the discretion the statute gave the Defense Minister down to nothing, holding that the sweeping yeshiva-student deferment ("Torato Omanuto" — some 8% of the draft cohort) was a "primary arrangement" only the Knesset may set. Unelected justices thus stripped a decades-old practice of its legal basis, suspending the ruling 12 months to force the elected legislature to act.


**Statute:** The blanket yeshiva-student ('Torato Omanuto') military-service deferment arrangement as administered by the Minister of Defense under the Defense Service Law [Consolidated Version], 5746-1986 (i.e., the deferment granted by executive/administrative discretion rather than by primary legislation)  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/rubinstein-v-minister-defense)


### HCJ 5100/94 (consolidated with HCJ 4054/95, 6536/95, 5188/96, 7563/97, 7628/97, 1043/99) — Public Committee Against Torture in Israel v. Government of Israel (1999)

*Read down / gutted*


The GSS leaned on the Criminal Procedure (Testimony) Ordinance and the Penal Law's necessity defense as legal cover for "moderate physical pressure" in interrogations. The Court voided no statute — it read them down to nothing: the power to interrogate authorizes no physical means, and necessity is only an after-the-fact defense for an individual, never advance authorization. Unelected justices thus hollowed out the legislative framework the regime relied on, leaving it powerless until the Knesset legislates expressly.


**Statute:** General Security Service (GSS/Shin Bet) interrogation authority — claimed under the general investigatory power (Criminal Procedure [Testimony] Ordinance, s.2(1)) combined with the necessity defense, Penal Law 5737-1977 s.34(11)  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/public-committee-against-torture-v-israel)


### CA (ע"א) 6024/97 — CA 6024/97 Shavit v. Rishon LeZion Jewish Burial Society (1999-07-06)

*Read down / gutted* · panel: President A. Barak, Justice M. Cheshin, Justice I. Englard (majority: Cheshin and Barak)


The Right to Alternative Civil Burial Law, 5756-1996 was enacted to widen burial choice — yet the Court refused to read it as overriding the Jewish burial societies whose rabbi barred a family from engraving Gregorian birth/death dates (in Latin numerals) alongside the Hebrew dates on a tombstone. Voiding neither statute nor bylaw, the majority (Cheshin, Barak) instead declared the societies dual public/private bodies bound by public-law norms, read the rule down, and recast the prohibition as an unconstitutional affront to the relatives' dignity — an unelected court hollowing a religious burial authority's rule-making into a shell while leaving its text formally intact.


**Statute:** Burial-society bylaws under religious burial authority; engaging Right to Alternative Civil Burial Law, 5756-1996; Standard Contracts Law; Basic Law: Human Dignity and Liberty  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/shavit-v-rishon-lezion-jewish-burial-society)


### CrimFH 7048/97 — Anonymous (Plonim, Lebanese detainees) v. Minister of Defense (2000)

*Read down / gutted*


The Knesset's Emergency Powers (Detentions) Law, 5739-1979, section 2, let the executive order administrative detention on state-security grounds. The Court voided no text — in a Further Hearing (Barak, 6-3) it read the section down to permit detention only where the detainee himself poses a danger, hollowing out the holding of Lebanese nationals as "bargaining chips" that the State had built on that same broad wording. Three unelected justices erased an entire executive policy without touching a word the legislature wrote.


**Statute:** Emergency Powers (Detentions) Law, 5739-1979, section 2  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/does-v-ministry-defense)


### HCJ 953/01 (consolidated with HCJ 1355/01, HCJ 7406/01, HCJ 2283/02) — HCJ 953/01 Solodkin v. Beit Shemesh Municipality (2004-06-14)

*Read down / gutted* · panel: Nine justices: Aharon Barak (President, author), Theodor Or, Eliahu Mazza, Mishael Cheshin, Jacob Turkel, Dorit Beinisch, Ayala Procaccia, Edmond E. Levy, Miriam Naor


The Local Authorities (Special Authorization) Law, 1956 empowered municipalities to ban pork sales — a grant that on its face permitted blanket prohibitions. Nine justices, led by Barak, struck nothing: they read the authorization down, construing it to require proportionate neighborhood-by-neighborhood balancing, suspended the Beit Shemesh, Carmiel and Tiberias by-laws and remanded them for redrafting — shrinking the power's real reach far below what the elected legislature had conferred, without voiding a word of the text.


**Statute:** Local Authorities (Special Authorization) Law, 5717-1956 — the statute empowering municipalities to enact by-laws restricting/prohibiting the sale of pork; and the municipal by-laws of Beit Shemesh, Carmiel and Tiberias issued under it.  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/solodkin-v-beit-shemesh-municipality)


### HCJ 2597/99; HCJ 2859/99 — HCJ 2597/99 & 2859/99 Rodriguez-Tushbeim v. Minister of Interior (2005-05-31)

*Read down / gutted* · panel: 11-justice panel: President Barak, Vice-President Emeritus Mazza, Vice-President Cheshin, Justice Emeritus Türkel, and Justices Beinisch, Rivlin, Procaccia, Levy, Grunis, Naor, Hayut. Vote 7-4 (majority: Barak, Mazza, Cheshin, Beinisch, Rivlin, Naor, Hayut; dissent: Türkel, Procaccia, Levy, Grunis).


Section 4B of the Law of Return, 5710-1950, leaves "converted" undefined, and the Interior Ministry read it to require converts to join and belong to the converting community before recognition as Jews. The Supreme Court (11-justice panel, 7-4, 2005) rejected the executive's narrowing gloss and held that conversion in a recognized Jewish community abroad followed by immigration suffices — an unelected court settling a charged value question and stretching the statute to cover non-Orthodox conversions past the line the elected administration sought to draw.


**Statute:** Law of Return, 5710-1950, sec. 4B (חוק השבות)  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/tais-rodriguez-tushbeim-v-minister-interior)


### CrimA 6659/06 (consolidated with CrimA 1757/07, 8228/07, and 3261/08) — A v. State of Israel (CrimA 6659/06) (2008)

*Read down / gutted*


The Knesset's Internment of Unlawful Combatants Law, 5762-2002 was built to permit indefinite detention on group-membership grounds, backed by a section 7 presumption that release endangers the state. The Court left the statute formally standing but hollowed it out — reading it to require proof of the detainee's own individual dangerousness on clear and convincing administrative evidence, rejecting bare membership, and demoting the presumption to a secondary step. An unelected court quietly converted the legislature's categorical group-detention regime into an individualized-threat one, never voiding a word.


**Statute:** Incarceration of Unlawful Combatants Law, 5762-2002 ("Internment of Unlawful Combatants Law")  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/v-state-israel-1)


### HCJ 6824/07 — Mana'e v. Tax Authority (מנאע נ' רשות המסים) (2010-12-20)

*Read down / gutted* · panel: President Dorit Beinisch, Justice Hanan Melzer, Justice Uzi Fogelman (opinion authored by Fogelman)


The Tax Authority routinely collected tax debts from citizens stopped at police checkpoints — and in malls and other public places — invoking the general collection powers of the Taxes (Collection) Ordinance, 1929. Without voiding a single word, the Court read those powers narrowly and held they contain no explicit authorization to collect a debt at a security roadblock; so unelected justices hollowed out an enforcement tool the legislature handed the executive, leaving the statutory text intact.


**Statute:** Tax Authority practice of collecting tax debts at police security roadblocks, asserted under general collection/seizure powers in the Tax (Collection) Ordinance and related tax statutes  

**Source:** [Wikipedia](https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D7%91%D7%92%22%D7%A5_%D7%9E%D7%A0%D7%90%D7%A2_%D7%A0%D7%92%D7%93_%D7%A8%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%AA_%D7%94%D7%9E%D7%A1%D7%99%D7%9D)


### HCJ 3809/08 (joined with HCJ 9995/08) — Association for Civil Rights in Israel v. Israel Police ('Big Brother' / Communications Data Law) (2012-05-28)

*Read down / gutted* · panel: Expanded 7-justice panel: President D. Beinisch (author), A. Grunis, E. Rivlin, M. Naor, E. Arbel, E. Hayut, H. Melcer


The Criminal Procedure (Enforcement Powers — Communications Data) Law, 5768-2007 — the "Big Brother" law the Knesset passed to grant police sweeping access to communications metadata. An expanded 7-justice panel under President Beinisch refused to strike it, yet read it so narrowly it left a shell: access was confined to a "focused and concrete purpose" rather than general crime detection, and warrantless access to extreme life-saving cases — an unelected court hollowing out the broad surveillance power the elected legislature intended, without voiding a single word of the text.


**Statute:** Criminal Procedure (Enforcement Powers — Communications Data) Law, 5768-2007 (חוק סדר הדין הפלילי (סמכויות אכיפה – נתוני תקשורת); 'חוק האח הגדול')

### HCJ 8091/14 — HaMoked: Center for the Defense of the Individual v. Minister of Defense (2014-12-31)

*Read down / gutted* · panel: Justice Elyakim Rubinstein (authoring), Justice Esther Hayut, Justice Noam Sohlberg


Regulation 119 of the Defence (Emergency) Regulations, 1945 — punitive house demolitions — is shielded by the preservation-of-laws clause, so the Court could not strike it. Instead, unelected justices held the power MUST be construed under Basic Law: Human Dignity and Liberty and exercised proportionately, imposing subtests (severity of offense, harm to innocent family members, strength of evidence, whether sealing or partial demolition would suffice) — converting a sweeping military demolition power into a constrained, individualized one barred from use as collective punishment, all without touching a word of the text.


**Statute:** Regulation 119 of the Defence (Emergency) Regulations, 1945 (תקנה 119 לתקנות ההגנה (שעת חירום)) — punitive house demolitions  

**Source:** [Versa · Cardozo](https://versa.cardozo.yu.edu/opinions/hamoked-center-defense-individual-v-minister-defense)


### בג"ץ 5239/11 (consolidated with 5392/11, 5549/11, 2072/12) — בג"ץ 5239/11 אורי אבנרי ואח' נ' הכנסת (Avneri v. Knesset (2015)

*Read down / gutted*


The Boycott Law (5771-2011) was built to deter: anyone publicly calling to boycott Israel is liable, and section 2(c) allowed compensation "without proof of damage." The elected Knesset legislated — and an unelected court struck 2(c) outright, then read the surviving tort down to demand proof of actual damage plus causation, hollowing out its deterrent bite and leaving the statute standing in name while neutralized in fact.


**Statute:** Prevention of Harm to the State of Israel by means of Boycott Law, 5771-2011 (החוק למניעת פגיעה במדינת ישראל באמצעות חרם, תשע"א-2011) — the civil-tort provision, section 2.

### בג"ץ 8101/15 / HCJ 8101/15 — בג"ץ 8101/15 צגטה נ' שר הפנים / HCJ 8101/15 Tsegeta (Tzegete) v. Minister of Interior (2017)

### HCJ 6494/14 (2016-06-06); Further Hearing דנג"ץ 5026/16 (2017-09-12) — HCJ 6494/14 & HCJ-FH (דנג"ץ) 5026/16 Gini v. Chief Rabbinate (גיני נ' הרבנות הראשית) (2017-09-12)

### HCJ 2109/20 (consolidated 2135/20, 2141/20) — Ben Meir v. Prime Minister (בן מאיר נ' ראש הממשלה) (2020-04-26)

The Government authorized the Shin Bet to track COVID patients' and contacts' cell-phone location data under Sec. 7(b)(6) of the GSS Law and emergency regulations issued under Basic Law: The Government — and the unelected Court, without formally voiding the tool, held it could no longer rest on cabinet/administrative authorization, set a short deadline, and forced the executive to dismantle its own emergency mechanism and move the program into primary Knesset legislation, draining the existing regulatory arrangement of effect without striking its text.

### HCJ 1550/18 (joined with HCJ 2240/18) — Secular Forum Association v. Minister of Health (עמותת הפורום החילוני נ' שר הבריאות) (2020-04-30)

### בג"ץ 11013/05 (HCJ 11013/05, consolidated petitions) — Reform/Conservative Conversions in Israel (consolidated petitions) v. Minister of the Interior (2021)
